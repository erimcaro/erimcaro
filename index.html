About

Eri is a language and linguistics nerd from the midwest USA who was raised on an unconventional combination of secular homeschooling, romance novels, and a relatively tame exchange year (if you know you know). In what turned out to be very good financial foresight, Eri left academia early and hasn't yet returned.


If not writing novels full-time, while parenting, during a pandemic, Eri would be back of house in a restaurant somewhere, dicing veggies to prog, post-punk, and reggaetón.



Eri is represented by Cheyenne Faircloth at Handspun Literary.

Books

Rears & Vices

HE THOUGHT HE’D BE A HERO BY NOW, AND THAT IT WOULD MEAN SOMETHING.
 It’s 1816. The wars with France and America are over. Royal Navy career man Everard Anderson de Anglada sails the peacetimes Great Lakes, demoted to captain of a tiny, ten-gun schooner. When Preston D’Arcy, Everard’s former lieutenant and too-handsome ex-flame, forewarns him about a courts martial they must judge, Everard is begrudgingly grateful.

HE’S RADICAL, RESPECTED, UNFORGETTABLE—AND A PIRATE.
On the docket, however, is Vitaliy (Vitya) Gray, American. Everard has crossed paths with him before—not strictly as enemies. Vitya’s charges mean he’ll hang. With quick thinking and quite a lot of perjury, Everard delays the trial. It costs him—and D’Arcy—everything: for Vitya’s true identity is V. Varfolomey, infamous pirate fleet captain and anti-colonial weapons smuggler. At least three crowns want him dead.

TOGETHER, THEY COULD BE LEGENDARY…
Between hasty jailbreak, philosophical debates, and proposals (pirate marriage, no-strings), Everard finds himself, his heart, and even D’Arcy commandeered: to the Gulf of México. There, piracy is nothing like he imagined, and Vitya is everything Everard ever truly wished to be.

…SO LONG AS LEGEND DOESN’T GET IN THE WAY OF LOVE.
As Everard struggles to regain his purpose in his new husband’s shadow, he must also reconcile the irresistible pulls of D’Arcy’s insistent affection and Vitya’s undemanding steadfastness. Then, with the Spanish crown looming, dangerous secrets and betrayals come to light, and Everard is offered a position with the revolutionary Galveston navy. Everard must decide: fulfill at long last his desire for legacy… or stay beside the men with whom he’s fallen in love, and make a legacy of their own.
